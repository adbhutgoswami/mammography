Tutorial 2 : MASSES-5

Lesions with Ill-defined Margins

As noted previously, a benign mass with its margin obscured by nearby tissue parenchyma may appear ill-defined. Nonetheless, where the mass is clearly visible, the finding that its margin is ill-defined may be indicative of tumor infiltration. While some benign masses such as cysts and fibroadenomas can have ill-defined margins, further investigations of a masses with ill-defined margins should be made because of its association with malignancy. Magnification view and spot compression may aid in clarifying the lesion's margin. Ultrasound will determine if the lesion is a cyst or not.
